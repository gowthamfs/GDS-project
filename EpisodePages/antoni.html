Episode 1

How a neurodiverse
individual can shine

Antoni Bohdanowicz

In our first episode of Global Dialogue
Series on Neurodiversity, we speak with
Antoni Bohdanowicz, who was diagnosed
with Asperger’s Syndrome at the age 16.

Since 2013, Antoni has been working as a journalist and is one of the leading voices of Autism advocacy in Poland. In 2019, Antoni joined BP in their Budapest office and is actively involved in various initiatives to nurture Neurodiversity within the company.Outside of BP, Antoni is helping to build ‘Neurodiversity in Business’, a forum for corporates to share their knowledge and experience in creating neuro-inclusive workspaces.

Antoni talks about the struggles he faced throughout his education, how it can be difficult for neurodiverse individuals when the system is not inclusive or adaptive to individual needs and how while he struggled in group classes, he excelled with individual tutoring.

Antoni highlights a really interesting point of how we should implement an education system that focuses on creating a personalized learning environment that can foster one's strengths and interests.

which I too personally believe is very important, especially for neurodiverse individuals. We have seen with so many of our own students at HashHackCode.

when we build the student’s interest, their confidence is boosted and we are then able to impart more advanced learning.A major struggle for many neurodiverse individuals is the job interview process.There are various reasons for this. Antoni shares a few of his own struggles of how the concept of an interview can build anxiety and how this can be tackled by providing the individuals the interview questions beforehand.

Antonie also shares his thoughts on how we should hire beyond a university degree as many neurodiverse individuals are not able to attend university as it is not accessible to them. What’s interesting in all of this is how Antoni shares that this process should be open for everyone and not just neurodiverse individuals or a segment of people, only then can we bring in true inclusion.

He also states that we should hire the best candidate for the role and not hire for the sake of diversity hiring, as they won't be able to grow in their job with meaningful sense in future.

During the episode, we also talk about the importance of the managers & team leaders, how they should understand that working processes are different for different people and have regular reviews to see what they are going through.

This can radically help reduce meltdowns. Showing empathy will not only help others but will also increase the productivity and innovation of the company.Initiatives are important to help diversity. Knowledge should be open so that awareness can build for all and have a global implementation model.

That's exactly what Antoni & co are working towards with “Neurodiversity in Business”, an open forum where experts are sharing their model and leading the way for all businesses to become more inclusive and understanding for neurodiverse individuals.
